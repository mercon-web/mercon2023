MERCon 2016 solicits research papers describing significant & innovative research contributions to all aspects of engineering. We invite submissions on a wide range of research topics, spanning both theoretical & systems research.

In addition to the main conference, MERCon will also co-host "ERU Symposium" – MERCon's predecessor aimed at stimulating and fostering student research. "MERCon-ERU Symposium" accepts only the extended abstracts. By the time of submission authors can indicate whether they are submitting the paper for MERCon or ERU Symposium.

Please note that survey/review papers will not be accepted.

MERCon Paper Submissions

All accepted and presented full papers to be submitted for publication to the IEEE Xplore.

  • All papers should be written in English with following maximum paper lengths:
    • 6 printed A4 pages.
  • Use the following template while preparing full papers:
    • Micorosft Word (.doc) format.
    • LaTex format - Zip, Tar
    • LaTex (Bibliography Files) - Zip, Tar

Use the following guidelines while submitting full papers:

  • All paper submissions are through EasyChair.
    • Please note that 1st time users need to create a new EasyChair account.
  • Manuscript submission should be in Adobe Portable Document Format (PDF) only.
  • Manuscripts that do not conform to the above formatting guidelines will not be considered.
  • We request all authors to carefully revise the manuscript for language and formatting.
  • All submitted manuscript will undergo a plagiarism check using a suitable tool.
  • Please contact us, if you have any questions regaring your submission or experience any issues while submitting your manuscript.

MERCon strongly discourages changes and, in particular, withdrawals of papers once submitted and included in the program. To avoid the likelihood of this, authors are strongly encouraged to get all necessary company and/or government approvals prior to submitting the paper to the conference.
If under any circumstances it becomes necessary for the author(s) to withdraw or change a paper, MERCon policy dictates that the request to do this must come directly from the author(s) and not from any third party. The author(s) will be held liable for all costs that are incurred. It would then be up to the author(s) to get reimbursed for the expense from any third party if he/she belives it is justified.

Instructions to Prepare IEEE Compliant PDF of Papers

Use the following instructions to prepare IEEE compliant PDF version of your paper (this is compulsory for all accepted papers):

  • Visit IEEE PDF eXpress website.
  • Create your PDF eXpress Account:
    • Log in to the IEEE PDF eXpress Plus site.
    • First-time users should do the following:
      1. Select the New Users - Click Here link.
      2. Enter the following:
        • 38401XP for the Conference ID
        • your email address
        • a password
      3. Continue to enter information as prompted.
      4. An Online confirmation will be displayed and an email confirmation will be sent verifying your account setup.
    • Previous users of PDF eXpress or IEEE PDF eXpress Plus need to follow the above steps, but should enter the same password that was used for previous conferences. Verify that your contact information is valid.
  • Then use IEEE PDF eXpress to either create a new PDF file using your source file (e.g., .docx) or verify the PDF that you already prepared.

Instructions for Camera Ready Paper Submission

1. Formatting Camera ready paper

Final camera-ready paper submissions must be limited to 6 pages accommodating reviewers' comments. Please DO NOT include page numbers in final submission. Use style files provided in IEEE Manuscript Templates for Conference Proceedings. Papers with formatting that does not exactly follow the template will not be accepted by IEEE Xplore and cannot be published. The appropriate copyright clearance code notice is to appear on the bottom, left-hand side of the first page of each paper, as follows:

  • For papers in which all authors are employed by the US government, the copyright notice is: U.S. Government work not protected by U.S. copyright
  • For papers in which all authors are employed by a Crown government (UK, Canada, and Australia), the copyright notice is: 978-1-5090-0645-8/16/$31.00 ©2016 Crown
  • For papers in which all authors are employed by the European Union, the copyright notice is: 978-1-5090-0645-8/16/$31.00 ©2016 European Union
  • For all other papers the copyright notice is: 978-1-5090-0645-8/16/$31.00 ©2016 IEEE
MS Word users can insert the appropriate copyright notice in a textbox, and place the box (without border) at the bottom left on the first page. LaTeX users may refer an example code as follows to add before the “\maketitle” code line. “\IEEEoverridecommandlockouts \IEEEpubid{\makebox[\columnwidth]{978-1-5090-0645-8/16/\$31.00~\copyright2016 IEEE \hfill} \hspace{\columnsep}\makebox[\columnwidth]{ }}”

See an example here.

2. IEEE Copyright Form

IEEE policy requires that prior to publication all authors or their employers must transfer to the IEEE in writing any copyright they hold for their individual papers. Authors of accepted paper(s) must submit completed copyright form(s) via email to mercon@uom.lk.

Download the blank IEEE copyright form and enter “2016 Moratuwa Engineering Research Conference (MERCon)” as the IEEE PUBLICATION TITLE.

See an example here.

3. Camera Ready Paper Submission

Please edit your paper by incorporating all reviewers' comments and use IEEE conference paper format. Make sure your paper is IEEE PDF compliant (recommend to use IEEE PDF eXpress). Please note that organizers reserve the right to reject your paper, if you don't modify your paper accordingly.

Please email the camera ready paper to mercon@uom.lk by Mar 28, 2016. Further make sure to email completed IEEE copyright form with the camera ready paper. Note: Please include “Camera Ready Paper - [paper No.]” in subject of your email.

MERCon Paper Presentations

  • Each paper is assigned a total of 15 min.
    • 12 min for presentation
    • 3 min for Q&A
    • Please keep these time limitations in mind when preparing your presentation.
  • Presenters are asked to arrive at their session 10 minutes prior so the Session Chair can confirm who is presenting the paper and that they are present.
  • Time limit will be strictly enforced due to the large number of papers in each session.
  • We recommend one of the authors to present the entire paper.
  • Each room will contain a multimedia projector and a laptop with Microsoft PowerPoint, Impress, and PDF reader installed.
  • Presenters may bring their own laptop or use the laptop provided in the conference room. However, please be sure to preview your presentation with the projector before starting your session.
  • If you need any assistance, please ask the organizing staff.
  • Papers that are not presented will not be send to IEEE Xplore for publication.


ERU Symposium Posters

  • Posters are presented during the Morning and Afternoon Tea Breaks on April 5.
  • At least one authors is expected to be present at the poster during these timeslots to answer any questions auidiance may have.
  • A poster can be up to 4' x 3' (1.22m x 0.91m) in size.
  • Make sure font size and illustrations are large enough to be seen from a meter or so.
  • Authors need to print and bring their own posters. Conference will give push pins and boards to attach posters.
  • All posters must be necessarily mounted before 9:00am on April 5. They should remain mounted until the end of the afternoon Tea session.
  • The posters in each session will be numbered. The list of numbers will be posted in the lobby. Please use your numbered location.
